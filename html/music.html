Compositions

Brother John: Two-Part Invention No. 1 in Bach’s Style

This was my first attempt at composing a two-part invention, using the first motif of Frère Jacques as a starting point. Inspired by Bach’s Invention No.1, I experimented with imitation, inversion, and harmonic shifts while keeping both voices clear and independent.

Brother John: Two-Part Invention No. 2 in Bach’s Style

With the second motif of Frère Jacques, I wanted to create something more energetic. This invention moves faster with dynamic sequences that drive the momentum forward.

Brother John: Chorale in Bach’s Style

Inspired by Bach’s four-part chorales and the Frère Jacques theme, this composition explore four-part harmony and voice leading. I transformed the Frère Jacques melody into four-part harmonic piece, focusing on smooth voice movement and balanced counterpoint.

Melody in F major

This composition explores a melody in F Major, incorporating an added 9th to create a richer, more expansive harmonic texture. The added 9th introduces a subtle tension that resolves beautifully, creating a sense of movement and anticipation.
